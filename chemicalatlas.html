Chemical Atlas

Digital prototype, 2025

Chemical Atlas is an art-science project that explores how chemical pollution can be understood and communicated through cartography and digital storytelling. Commissioned by researchers at UCL, the project responds to the difficulty of mapping pollution across different geographic and temporal scales. Rather than functioning as a comprehensive atlas, the current interface (v1.0) was designed as a working prototype, that can be used in future research. It is available open source on GitHub

Process

Sabin facilitated a co-design workshop at UCL in 2025 with researchers and practitioners working across social sciences, medicine, chemistry, arts, and journalism. She then designed and developed the prototype, which is built in HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. Chemical Atlas draws on the visual language of vintage geology texts, deliberately holding that aesthetic in tension with the project's critical, postcolonial framing.
