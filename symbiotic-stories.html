Symbiotic Stories

spudWORKS Residency and Manifesto


In Summer 2022, I joined forces with critical designers Nirit Binyamini Ben Meir and Liina Lember for a one-week collaborative residency at spudWORKS art gallery. Set against the New Forest National Park, one of the remaining commons in the British Isles, we embarked on a period of peer-led learning and creative experimentation.

Slowing down to tune into how we relate to our surroundings and the notion of the commons, we sketched, wrote, and shared emerging ideas. This process of inquiry and reflection led to the creation of a manifesto, which we shared with Information Experience Design (IED) students at the Royal College of Art the following Summer.

We approached the topic of symbiosis as a prompt for rethinking home environments as shared with microbial and more-than-human communities. Our experiments with AI image generation allowed us to rapidly generate visuals that could then catalyse a discussion about commensual kinships across species.

This humble collaboration continues to have a lasting impact on my approach to artistic research. We are grateful to spudWORKS for hosting and a-n The Artist's Information Company for supporting this project through their bursary program.
